## What Went Well

- The team accepted that Epic 9 v1 had become too patch-heavy and executed a clean v2 rewrite instead of continuing to stack exceptions.
- The canonical deployment architecture document became a real source of truth. Stories, docs, manifests, scripts, and validation now point back to it.
- Review findings materially improved the work. Examples include Zot bootstrap ordering, htpasswd safety, Dapr annotation patch scope, JWT `secretKeyRef` creation, no-decode Docker auth validation, parser redaction, and LiveCluster outcome assertions.
- The deployment surface now has layered evidence: Kustomize, static lint, script behavior tests, fixture-based tests, poison redaction checks, and build gate checks.
- The team kept Kubernetes ownership boundaries clearer than in v1. Aspirate owns the seven application service manifests, Dapr CRs are hand-authored, and Redis/Keycloak are explicit carve-outs.
- Story 9.8 converted a recurring "known unrelated full-suite failure" pattern into a clean-clone build policy and a regression guard.

John (Product Manager): "The major product improvement is confidence. Operators now have a named platform path instead of tribal knowledge."

Winston (System Architect): "The best decision was the reset. It gave every artifact an owner and stopped the old story numbering from driving current architecture."

## Challenges

- The Epic 9 v1 and v2 overlap creates traceability noise. Historical story files and the older Epic 9 retrospective remain useful, but they are no longer the current source of truth.
- Some assumptions in the story drafts were wrong or incomplete. Aspirate flags, MinVer resolution, `PUBLISH_TARGET`, `ContainerImageTags`, and `.NET` roll-forward behavior all needed correction through implementation.
- Patch idempotency was harder than it looked. Several early checks relied on presence or substring anchors where the final behavior needed create-or-upsert logic and postcondition verification.
- Full-solution validation was noisy across several stories before Story 9.8. That made focused acceptance evidence necessary, but also risked normalizing "unrelated failure" language.
- LiveCluster evidence remains opt-in and was not executed in the recorded Story 9.7 closure because no sandbox kubeconfig was provided.
- Several deployment risks remain intentionally deferred: Zot operational hardening, Redis production hardening, dual-mode AppHost simplification, Keycloak URL source-of-truth cleanup, and shell guard portability.

Murat (Test Architect): "The recurring testing lesson is that static text checks are useful but not sufficient. The best review fixes moved assertions to behavior: second-pass patching, temp workspaces, poison values, and residual probes."

Paige (Technical Writer): "The recurring documentation lesson is that a canonical doc helps only when every entry-point doc is checked against it."

## Key Lessons

1. Greenfield replanning can be the lower-risk path when correction history becomes the design.
   Evidence: The approved Epic 9 rewrite replaced fragmented v1 planning with the v2 deployment platform narrative and reset physical artifacts.

2. Deployment safety must be executable, not only documented.
   Evidence: Stories 9.5 through 9.7 turned context confirmation, Secret handling, Dapr order, lint categories, and redaction into script and test behavior.

3. Idempotency needs postcondition checks.
   Evidence: Review patches repeatedly replaced "already present" assumptions with create-or-upsert logic and verification of final YAML or cluster-facing state.

4. Security-sensitive tooling must avoid value echo by construction.
   Evidence: Story 9.5 and Story 9.6 hardened parser diagnostics, Docker auth handling, Secret bootstrap output, and poison assertion failures.

5. Source-of-truth boundaries need active guards.
   Evidence: AppHost, Dapr CRs, and Redis/Keycloak carve-outs each own different deployment slices. Where one artifact knows facts from another, the team had to add anchors or tests.

6. Optional live-cluster tests are valuable only when their absence is explicit.
   Evidence: Story 9.7 records LiveCluster tests as skipped when `KUBECONFIG_TEST_PATH` is absent, rather than letting default validation imply live deployment proof.
