## What Went Well

- Deployment validation became concrete and CI-friendly. The PowerShell validator reports specific misconfigurations, supports console and JSON output, and distinguishes production failures from local-development warnings.
- DAPR configuration coverage moved beyond pub/sub. Epic 8 added production state store templates and checked access control, scoping, dead letters, resiliency targets, and connection-string handling.
- Health and readiness semantics became operationally useful. `/health`, `/ready`, and `/alive` now distinguish sidecar, state store, pub/sub, and projection actor behavior instead of relying on placeholder self-checks.
- Graceful degradation behavior became visible to clients and operators through headers, ProblemDetails responses, health status, and runbook guidance.
- Projection lifecycle handling matured sharply. Story 8.3 added corruption detection, rebuild status, manual rebuild, checkpoint resume, manifest fallback, and degraded query behavior during rebuild.
- Review feedback found real defects before closure. Examples include wildcard access-control validation, local-development profile behavior, circular health-check middleware paths, projection actor health serialization sensitivity, and rebuild checkpoint gaps.
- The team reused existing boundaries well. EventStore stayed responsible for event persistence and replay foundations; Parties added the operational surface needed for its own read models and deployment assets.

Alice (Product Owner): "The product win is confidence. Operators can now validate, observe, and recover the service rather than infer behavior from architecture notes."

Winston (Architect): "The architecture win is traceability. D14 and D15 are no longer theory; they have services, endpoints, tests, and docs."

## Challenges

- Static DAPR validation had profile-sensitive behavior. Production security rules and AppHost local-development rules look similar structurally but require different pass/fail behavior.
- PowerShell validation had cross-platform and Windows-specific pitfalls. Strict mode, collection counts, argument parsing, and dependency-light YAML parsing all needed care.
- Runtime health checks exposed real middleware and actor-system edge cases. Health checks running through actor paths created a circular call pattern until the middleware skipped actor routes.
- Projection actor health initially depended on payload serialization that was not safe for health probing. The fix was to use remoting-safe `PingAsync()` probes.
- Tier 3 outage testing hit topology limits. Pub/sub-only failure cannot be isolated in the in-memory DAPR topology because components share the sidecar process.
- Rebuild implementation had deeper state-access complexity than planning implied. DAPR actor state required HTTP API access, and tenant-wide index recovery needed manifest fallback when the index itself is corrupted.
- Some acceptance criteria depended on platform behavior. The team had to document where EventStore owns crash recovery and where Parties can only verify readiness and sidecar recovery behavior.

Dana (QA Engineer): "The repeated pattern is that operational features need adversarial tests. Happy-path health and rebuild logic would have missed several production-facing defects."

Charlie (Senior Dev): "Agreed. The story records show that review remediation was not cosmetic. It changed behavior."

## Key Lessons

1. Operational readiness is a product feature, not deployment decoration.
   Evidence: Epic 8 added validation output, health status, degraded headers, admin rebuild, and runbook guidance that operators directly depend on.

2. Health checks must avoid exercising unsafe or recursive paths.
   Evidence: Story 8.2 fixed actor-route middleware recursion and replaced projection payload reads with `PingAsync()` probes.

3. Static validation needs environment-aware policy.
   Evidence: Story 8.1 had to distinguish production access-control failures from acceptable local AppHost warnings.

4. Rebuild capability needs its own recovery data.
   Evidence: Story 8.3 added resumable checkpoints and index manifest persistence so rebuild does not depend solely on corrupted projection state.

5. Degradation must be explicit to callers.
   Evidence: Story 8.2 and 8.3 added `X-Service-Degraded` and stale-data headers for read paths, while write failures map to dependency-aware ProblemDetails.

6. Story notes must separate verified behavior from inherited platform guarantees.
   Evidence: Story 8.2 kept full event replay as EventStore responsibility and recorded only the sidecar recovery behavior that Parties validated.

## Technical Debt And Carry-Forward Items

## Significant Discovery Check

Amelia (Developer): "Epic 8 surfaced several discoveries that matter for Epic 9."

Significant discovery 1:

- The deployment validator warns about missing secret-store configuration but does not yet prove Epic 9 key-management readiness end to end.

Impact:

- Epic 9 should treat secret-store validation as a hard closure concern once per-party keys are active.

Significant discovery 2:

- Projection recovery depends on checkpoint and manifest data, not only event replay.

Impact:

- Erasure verification must ensure auxiliary recovery data does not preserve or reintroduce erased personal data.

Significant discovery 3:

- Full-topology testing has component-isolation limits.

Impact:

- Epic 9 should be explicit about which privacy and erasure guarantees are proven by unit, integration, topology, or platform-level tests.

Significant discovery 4:

- Health and degradation behavior can accidentally exercise sensitive paths if probes use real query payloads.

Impact:

- GDPR health and verification probes should use minimal, non-personal, remoting-safe signals wherever possible.

Winston (Architect): "These do not invalidate Epic 9. They sharpen the acceptance bar: no personal-data lifecycle can depend on undocumented or unverified operational assumptions."

Alice (Product Owner): "The product implication is simple: GDPR cannot be mostly correct. The last Epic 9 review should focus on closure evidence."

Epic update required: No fundamental Epic 9 direction change is required. Epic 9 closure should explicitly validate secret-store configuration, projection cleanup, subscriber notification, and search/temporal projection behavior against the operational capabilities delivered in Epic 8.
